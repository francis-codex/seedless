## 1. Positioning

Seedless is a passkey wallet on Solana built for people who get paid in crypto but do not live in crypto.

No seed phrase. No gas. Face ID or fingerprint in, money out. The wallet is a tool for moving and receiving stablecoins, not a destination for trading or staking. Every product decision is filtered through that lens.

The category is consumer payments, not DeFi infrastructure.


## 2. Audience

The target user is the crypto-paid worker who treats their wallet as a checking account, not a portfolio.

Concrete segments:

- Engineers, designers, and operators at remote-first crypto-native companies paid in USDC or stablecoins.
- Founders and team members in emerging markets (Nigeria first, broader Africa and LATAM next) where local banking rails are friction-heavy.
- Solana ecosystem participants who currently rely on a seed-phrase wallet they distrust and a custodial exchange they distrust differently.

The shared behavior across these segments is identical: receive stablecoins, hold briefly, send to someone or convert to local currency. Self-custody without the cognitive tax.

## Why Now

The thesis behind Seedless is that the next wave of Solana growth in emerging markets does not look like the last one. It is not DeFi yield seekers and it is not NFT speculators. It is the engineer in Lagos getting paid in USDC who needs to settle rent in naira on the same day. It is the operator in Buenos Aires whose company switched payroll to stablecoins this quarter. It is the designer in Manila who is tired of explaining seed phrases to her mother.

For that user, the wallet has to be mobile-first, passkey-secured, and shipped with the offramp story already live in the first market that needs it. Seedless has built that wallet. Mainnet is live. The audit is in place. The partner stack and the distribution lane are in place. The remaining question is which partners lean in for the first launch window and which wait to see week-one numbers. Seedless is asking Solana Foundation to be in the first group.
