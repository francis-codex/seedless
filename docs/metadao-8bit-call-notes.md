## What 8bitpenis Said

### The Problem with Most Tokens
Most crypto tokens don't represent anything. They're not connected to the company. When companies get acquired (Tensor by Coinbase, Padre by Pump), token holders get abandoned because there's no legal protections. Even strong projects like Meteora — great product, great revenue — token still struggles because there's no connection between token and company.

### What Ownership Coins Solve
Ownership coins give you legal and economic ownership of the company. Like stocks — if the company does well, the token follows. The token is legally tied to the treasury and IP. Investors get real rights, including liquidation rights. If the founder runs away, there's actual legal ground to stand on.

### Futarchy Governance (How It Works)
- Treasury is controlled by governance, not the founder
- You get a monthly allowance (salary + infrastructure) you can spend freely
- For anything else, you put up a proposal (takes ~3 days)
- Proposals can only pass if traders believe it will make the token price go up
- You cannot pass a proposal that would hurt the project
- These are essentially prediction markets for decisions ("decision markets")
