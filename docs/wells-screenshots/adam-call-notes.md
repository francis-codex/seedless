## What Adam Said

### The product is directionally correct, but it reads as a generic crypto wallet.
It's not differentiated enough. If someone looks at Seedless right now, they don't immediately understand why it exists or who it's for. It looks like another wallet.

### "Non-crypto-native" is not a specific enough target user.
Phantom has already done well at onboarding non-crypto-native users. Saying "we're for people who don't use crypto" doesn't tell anyone anything. Everyone says that. You need a much more opinionated, narrow target user.

### Make Nigeria and Africa the headline, not a footnote.
The M-Pesa integration for Kenya, the on-the-ground presence in Nigeria — that's the angle. That's what makes Seedless different. Right now it's buried. It should be the first thing people see.

### The website reads like it's for developers, not consumers.
"Privacy infrastructure on Solana" sounds B2B. It sounds like a dev tool. The people you're trying to reach don't know what infrastructure means in this context. Look at Phantom, Family Wallet, FOMO — friendly, inviting, consumer-facing design. That's the bar.

### You're too focused on building, not enough on distribution.
Features don't raise money. Proven demand raises money. You can have the best product in the world but if nobody is using it, investors don't care. Go talk to users before the product is finished. Find out what they actually need.

### X (Twitter) is not where your target users are.
If your users are in Lagos and Ibadan, go to Lagos and Ibadan. Go offline. Go door to door. City by city. That's where the distribution happens for the audience you're targeting.

### Solo founder is a red flag.
It's bearish for investors. Find a co-founder, someone who complements your skills — ideally someone on the distribution/business side. Superteam Nigeria is a good place to look.

### Proven demand makes fundraising easy. Features alone don't.
Don't come to raise money with a feature list. Come with 100 active wallet users and stories about what you learned from them. That's what makes investors say yes.
